01

The White Revolution

Sparked by Tribhuvandas Patel and Dr. Verghese Kurien in Anand, Gujarat, Amul transformed India from a milk-deficient nation into the world's largest milk producer.

A cooperative movement that empowered millions of dairy farmers.

02

Utterly Butterly Delicious!

Introduced in 1966, the iconic Amul Girl with her polka-dotted dress and blue hair captured the nation's imagination. Her witty, satirical topicals remain India's longest-running outdoor advertising campaign.

"Utterly Butterly Delicious, Amul!"
03

Uncompromising Quality

Every pack of Amul butter represents the highest standards of safety, quality, and rich flavor. Churned from the freshest cream, it is the golden standard of taste in every Indian household.

04

Owned by 3.6 Million Farmers

Amul is not just a brand; it is a farmer-owned institution. 100% of the profits flow directly back to the milk producers, fueling rural growth and economic independence across India.
